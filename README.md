# 🎮 PICO-8 Projects

A collection of small games and experiments built in [PICO-8](https://www.lexaloffle.com/pico-8.php) as I learn Lua and game development from the ground up.

---

## 👾 About

I'm using PICO-8 as my learning environment — its constraints make it a great place to focus on fundamentals without getting lost in tooling or overscoping projects (see all my rpg maker, unity, and unreal attempts haha). Each project here is hopefully a step forward in understanding Lua, game logic, project management, and design.

My main interest is **RPGs**. The games that shaped what I love about the genre come from the NES, SNES, Sega Genesis, and Game Boy / GBA eras — that sweet spot of 8 bit to 16 bit sprite-based worlds, turn-based systems, and stories told within tight hardware limits. PICO-8 feels like a natural fit for exploring what makes those games tick. As well as help me not take several years per project. 

---

## 🗡️ Focus: RPGs

The classics that inspire this work:

- **NES** — Final Fantasy I & III, Dragon Quest I-IV, Shell Monster Story
- **SNES** — Earthbound, Lufia II, 7th Saga, Breath of Fire, Super Mario RPG, Final Fantasy VI
- **Genesis** — Phantasy Star I-IV, Shining Force
- **Game Boy / GBA** — Pokémon, Final Fantasy Legend I-III, Knight Quest, Mother 3

The goal isn't to recreate these games — it's to understand the systems underneath them: overworld maps, turn-based combat, dialogue trees, choice driven stories, inventory management, character growth systems, save states — and rebuild + reimagine those ideas while adding my own experimental ideas in PICO-8. My hope is to
eventually move the ideas that I feel really work into larger projects in Love2d but the goal is to get comfortable quickly building systems and iterating on them.

## 🛠️ What I'm Learning

- **Lua** — syntax, tables, functions, state management
- **Game loops** — update / draw cycles, delta time, input handling
- **RPG systems** — combat, stats, dialogue, maps, saves
- **PICO-8 constraints** — 128×128 display, 16 colors, 8KB cart limit, sprite/map/sfx tools, efficient coding
